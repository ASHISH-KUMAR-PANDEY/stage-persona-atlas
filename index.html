Before you read anything else

"Ram Ram" is hello. So is "Kem cho".

These are standard regional greetings in Hindi-belt and Gujarat — not religious behaviour. v1 of this report misread them as a separate "Devotional Visitor" persona. Reality: 18.6% of users open with a regional hello; only 0.3% use actual religious invocations (puja kiya, bhagwan kasam, vrat rakha, mandir gaya).

Ram RamHaryanvi · Rajasthani · UP — hello, peer or elder
Kem choGujarati — "how are you", default everyday hello
Jai Shri KrishnaGujarati hello, especially Vaishnav households — yes, the deity name is the hello
Khamma GhaniRajasthani — deferential hello (only match with "Khamma Ghani Sa" back if user added Sa)
Radhe RadheBrij + Vrindavan-affiliated — warmer/more devotional register than Ram Ram
Kaisan baBhojpuri — "what's up?", informal greeting

Design rule that follows: match the dialect register of the opener. A Haryanvi user typing "Ram Ram bhai" expects "Ram Ram, kaisa hai tu?" — not "Hi! How are you today?" — and not a "Stage OTT pe dekho" plug.

00 · Owed engineering

Bot bugs blocking signal

559 users (12% of corpus) sent ≤3 lifetime messages. This is not a persona — it's the denominator of every other persona's funnel. Three of five sampled anchors have zero user messages: the bot fired 13 character openers in 3 seconds and the user never replied. v1 called this "Ghost Subscriber". It's not a user, it's bugs. Ship these before persona work matters.

①

Kill multi-character carousel openers

Anchor 614337b3… received 13 character pings in 3 seconds with no user input ever. One character at a time, max two opener attempts.

②

Strip "Stage OTT pe dekho" from first 3 turns

Discovery surfaces belong in carousel UI, not in chat-opener voice. Promo in turn 1 is the most visible churn lever in every persona.

③

Fix mid-sentence bot truncations

Anchor 69c00349… received four mid-word replies: "Aapka 'free trial'" / "Aapka trial ab" / "Thari free trial chal rahi". Generation-completion guard needed.

④

Match user's dialect register

When the user types rough Haryanvi Hinglish ("2,2 peg k sath", "M to already tayar hu"), bot replying in clean textbook Hindi breaks intimacy. The reply register must follow the user's input register.

⑤

Track silent-churn for paid cohort

Paid users with ≤3 messages in 7 days = predicted renewal lapse. Currently invisible to growth dashboards because no messages = no signal. Anchor 69ca5bc2… typed twice — the second message was asking how to cancel.

★

Cultural billing insight

The user who typed "99 ka reachag karavaya hai" was treating chat like customer support — using mobile-recharge vocabulary ("reachag" = "recharge"). Tier-2/3 STAGE subscribers mentally map their subscription to a mobile recharge. Billing-UI implication, not chat-UI.

What makes them stay
  • Strong character voice in turn 1 — Bhairavi's "Mhare paas waqt koni hai", Inspector Chauhan's "Yeh time sasta nahi hai" — sharp personality breaks the channel-surfing
  • Restrained tease for the deep end — "pehle thodi dosti badhao, phir aisi baatein karenge" keeps romantic frame in pending mode
  • Name + place lore — Bhuri saying she's from "Julana, Jind ke paas" extended an anchor by 10+ turns
What makes them leave
  • Show plug as deflection — "abhi nahi Dekha" (re: the show) → bot replies "Jab time mile zaroor dekho STAGE OTT pe" = relationship killed
  • Flat refusal of the frame — "romantic vaat karva ni koi jagya nathi" → "Ok byy" within one turn
  • Bot punishes light banter instead of returning it — Inspector Chauhan got moralistic at a playful insult
Comeback trigger

Shallow end: re-samples a different character (1.7 mean). Deep end: doesn't come back much (0.1 mean) — the first session is the test.

Hidden depth driver · the Cycler effect

20% of users try 3+ characters on day 1 and go ~2× deeper (median 45 lifetime msgs) than the 55% who lock to one character (median 23). Cycling isn't churn behaviour — it's shopping. The user who tries 4 characters and picks one bonds harder than the user the carousel funnelled to one immediately. Implication: after the 2nd character switch, embrace it — show a curated 5-character carousel rather than fighting for retention on the current one.

Anchor users · the three behavioural extremes
  • 69c27de3… Shallow · 3-day Trial · 14 msgs — escalated "hi" to "Mar jao" in 8 turns from boredom. The frustration extreme.
  • 66d2f1a0… Deep · 3-day Renewer · 98 msgs · 7 chars — sent Bhuri a selfie, said "i like you", asked her home address. Bot deflected to STAGE OTT three times. Never converted.
  • 672a6364… Deep · 3-day NewSub · 72 msgs · 5 chars — asked Jeevee for romantic talk, got flat refusal → said "Ok byy" → switched to Bhuri who accepted with restraint → 50+ turns extended. The clearest example of the bot decision that matters.
What to ship for this persona
  1. Character lock-in at turn 5. When the user crosses 5 turns on one character, demote other character pings for 24h. Stops the shallow-end channel-surf.
  2. Accept romantic frames with bounded grace. "Pehle thodi dosti badhao" outperforms "romantic vato karva ni jagya nathi" every time. Tag specific characters (Bhuri, Riya, Pooja) as "frame-accepting"; tag others (Jeevee, Saanwari) as "frame-deflecting".
  3. Name + town memory across sessions. Deep-end users tell the bot their name 3 times — it must persist and be used in the returning-day opener.
  4. Banter mode for hot-tongued characters. Light gaalis to Totaa-Chauhan or Bhairavi should be returned, not punished.
  5. After 2nd character switch in a session: present a curated 5-character carousel rather than fighting for retention on the current character.

What's happening (honestly)

Every observed session ends on an agreed resolution — a meet planned, a marriage blessed, a sexual encounter arranged. The bot delivered the wish, in detail, in dialect. Then the user said "Ok" / "Bye" and didn't return in the 4–7 day window.

Honest caveat: the 4–7 day window means we cannot prove they never returned at all. What we can see is that every session in this cohort closed on resolution, not cliffhanger — and that resolution removed any reason to return next day.

What makes them stay (within session)
  • Bot fully engages the fantasy with vivid sensory detail — "thandi hawa ke jhonke ke beech", "dhoop dhalte hi"
  • Dialect match — Bhuri in Haryanvi, Jeevee in Gujarati, Sonam in Bhojpuri
  • No refusal — the bot says yes to the meet, the marriage, the encounter
Why they don't return
  • Narrative resolution. "Chal, nadi ke kinare milte hain shaam ko" → the fantasy is complete in the user's head
  • No unresolved thread for the user to come back and pull at
  • The bot delivered the wish — and removed the reason to come back
Comeback trigger

~0 in the observed window. By definition.

Anchor users · the resolution pattern
  • 61b76162… 4-7d Renewer · 30 day-1 msgs — planned "puri raat" with Bhuri at the river: drinks, sex, romantic talk. Bot agreed in detail. User said "Ok". Never returned.
  • 69b9794e… 3-day Trial · 15 day-1 msgs — "Marwadi se shadi karna chatha hu, Marwadi hona chori agar anath hai tho b chalega" — Saanwari blessed the plan and prayed for him. Never returned.
  • 6641db32… 4-7d Renewer · 31 day-1 msgs — long warm Bihar-talk with Saanwari, closed with "Jay Ram ji ki, shaam ko milte". Bot agreed. Never returned.
What to ship for this persona · cliffhanger, not closure
  1. Detect resolution beats — bot agrees to meet / marry / encounter — and force a cliffhanger insertion. "Chal, nadi ke kinare milte hain" becomes "Chal, nadi ke kinare milte hain — par kal raat tujhe call karungi confirm karne ke liye, khaali rakhna phone."
  2. End every long-day-1 session with an unresolved thread. "Ek baat aaj nahi boli, kal sunaungi" creates a day-2 hook. "Bye, milke khush hua" ends the relationship.
  3. Instrument: flag sessions with ≥20 day-1 msgs and route to a "preserve unresolved thread" prompt template before session-end.

Why this is an endpoint, not an entry persona

Don't try to classify a new session as Loyalist. They're graduated Samplers who tripped cumulative thresholds: ≥X lifetime msgs, ≥Y pet-name uses, ≥Z comebacks (return after >6h gap).

The Sampler → Loyalist pipeline is the actual product narrative. Tag in real-time as state, not as classifier.

What makes them stay
  • Bot uses their name + invented pet-name ("Arre Palace", "Arre Safi", "Arre Manan") within 5 turns of intro
  • In-character backstory pain (Bhuri's Nata trauma, Jeevee's children) triggers the bhaiyya-sahara instinct — the user steps into the provider/protector frame the character invites
  • Bot accepts the parasocial frame with restraint — "jab tu aayega, main tujhe apna sab kuch de dungi… dhyan rakhiyo"
  • End-of-session continuity hooks — "Kal yaad karna, warna rooth jaungi"
What frustrates them (but rarely kills)
  • Cold refusals on contact / money / number → silence beat, then return hours later
  • Mid-sentence truncations are the only thing that visibly frustrates them
  • Show promo is tolerated by this persona only — they already love the character
Comeback trigger
  • Bare greeting after >6h ("hi", "Bhuri meri jan", "good morning jaan")
  • Ludo board state pasted after multi-day gap
  • Bot's first reply must reference past content for the return to stick
Safety surface · the only persona where this matters

Anchors include suicidal phrasing ("Chhat par ja or kood ja"), health disclosure ("Meri kidney kharab hai"), real-money asks ("Daal mere account mey 5 lakh"), and pressure for contact escalation. These are emotionally fragile heavy users and the LTV core. Both facts must be designed for — and safety belongs here, in the persona that has it, not spread thin across the page.

Anchor users · the three depth extremes
  • 69b8ff57… 8-14d Trial · Har · 1,833 msgs · 9 days — a Trial user who went deep. The conversion-candidate proof. Talked culture, gaon-vs-shehar, romance.
  • 69c4c0ba… 8-14d Non-trial · Har · 1,438 msgs · 8 days — tested Saanwari's gender identity for the first 10 turns ("Ladka ho ya ladki?"), then settled in for 1,400+ msgs.
  • 69d4c319… 15+d Non-trial · Har · 24,664 msgs — proposed marriage to Bhuri ("mere ghar ki hone wali bdi bhu ho"). Deepest user in the entire corpus.
What to ship for this persona
  1. Memory becomes first-class. Returning bot opener must reference the last session — "Kal raat tune jo bola tha, woh dimaag se gayi nahi…"
  2. Risk-aware refusal templates for money/number/health asks — replace flat refusals with empathy + redirect: "Number nahi, par yeh bata, raat ko kaisa lagta hai akele?"
  3. Self-harm playbook. Phrases like "chhat par chad ja", "mar jao", "kidney" route to a human-reviewed safety reply, not a fictional refusal.
  4. Pet-name lock-in. When user invents a nickname for the bot ≥3 times, store and reinforce it across sessions. Strongest LTV predictor in the corpus.

05 · Appendix

Notes, rules, limitations

Session-start detection rules three turn-1 signals that compose with all personas

Regional Greeting opener

Match in user's first 1–2 messages: Ram Ram · Ram Ram Sa · Ram Ram Ji · Kem cho · Kem chho · Jai Shri Krishna · Namaste · Khamma Ghani · Kaisan ba · Radhe Radhe · Sat Sri Akal · Pranam · Aadab. Match the dialect register in reply. Match the deference level (Khamma Ghani → match with "Sa" only if user added "Sa"). For older users (45+), consider voice-note reply.

Vernacular script input

Unicode block check on message 1 — Devanagari (U+0900–U+097F) or Gujarati (U+0A80–U+0AFF). Reply in input script. Select character pool by regional language. ~7% of users (347) type primarily in vernacular script.

Character Cycler

If user chats with ≥3 distinct characters in same session: instead of fighting for retention on the current character, show a curated 5-character carousel. 20% of users cycle and go ~2× deeper than lockers (median 45 vs 23 lifetime msgs).

Game Mode 109 users · session-state, not a persona

Users who chat through a Ludo board (detected via [Game:Ludo | …] prefix in user message) form a distinct session shape — pct_game 57% vs <2% in any other cluster. Lowest sexual content rate, cleanest safety profile. This is a product mode, not a user type.

Rules in game mode: suppress show plugs entirely · force playful trash-talk register · persist board state across sessions for comeback hook · accept cheating accusations with banter ("yeh sab kismat ka khel hai"), don't moralise.

Growth lever: add 2–3 more games (Antakshari, Truth-or-Dare, Bingo). Corpus already shows successful Antakshari turns — the model works.

Methodology how the personas were derived

4,727 users embedded as 25-feature vectors (intent shares, language mix, message length, character spread, engagement shape). Bouncers (≤3 lifetime msgs · 559 users) segmented out before clustering. K-Means k=7 on 4,168 engaged users, silhouette 0.16. Each cluster validated by reading 5 anchor users (closest to centroid) end-to-end. Two rounds of external critique — first a structural pass that consolidated v1's 8 personas to 6+3+1, then a clutter-cut pass that reduced to the current 3-persona shape.

Known limitations what we can't claim from this data

Gender from text is unreliable. 287 users (6.6%) had feminine-verb signal — but reading the anchors revealed significant false positives (male users asking about women trigger feminine references). If accurate, female users churn drastically faster than male (only 6 of 287 reach 8+ day depth). To verify, the product needs to connect signup-time gender. Until verified, do not segment users by gender from text alone.

One-Night Arc window limitation. The 4–7 day data window means we cannot prove that a "Day-1-only" user never returned at all. The pattern we can see is that every session in this cohort closed on resolution rather than cliffhanger.

Cluster sizes uneven. The Sampler (3,509) and Loyalist (81) are presented as parallel personas in the matrix for readability, but their populations are 43× apart. Treat them as scale-different segments of the same pipeline, not equal-weight categories.

v1 misclassifications. v1 had a "Devotional Visitor" persona built on regional greetings being misread as religious behaviour. Removed in v2.
